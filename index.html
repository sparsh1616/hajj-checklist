Ihram Rules Reminder

  • Men: 2 unstitched white sheets (rida + izar) — wear on plane or at Miqat
  • Women: Any modest, loose, full-coverage clothing — their normal abaya serves as ihram
  • Wear ihram before crossing Miqat (usually before landing in Jeddah)

Important Hajj Tips

  • Stay with your group at all times — crowds are massive
  • Drink Zamzam water regularly — it's blessed and refreshing
  • Rest when possible — Hajj is physically demanding
  • Keep small Saudi Riyals for tips and small purchases

Money Matters

  • Carry 500–1000 SAR per person for emergencies
  • Use Saudi Riyals; Indian Rupees not accepted
  • Forex cards work at ATMs in Saudi
  • Exchange some money at Indian airport for immediate needs

Safety Reminders

  • Photocopy all documents and keep separate from originals
  • Memorize your hotel name and group number in Arabic
  • Indian Haj Mission helpline: +966-XXXX-XXXXX
  • Mark luggage with unique ribbons for easy identification
  • Women: avoid carrying heavy bags — use rolling luggage

Health During Hajj

  • Wear mask in crowded areas — prevent respiratory infections
  • Apply sunscreen every 2–3 hours
  • Break in your walking shoes before the trip
  • Elderly pilgrims: take rest breaks every 30 minutes of walking
  • Report any fever immediately to medical camps
